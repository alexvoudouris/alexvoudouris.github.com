About me

I am a Lecturer (Assistant Professor) at the School of Computer Science and Electronic Engineering (CSEE) of the University of Essex, and a member of the Artificial Intelligence group, and of the Centre for Computational Finance and Economic Agents. Prior to this, I was a postdoctoral researcher at the Department of Computer Science of the University of Oxford, supervised by Edith Elkind. I obtained my PhD in computer science in September 2018 from the University of Patras, where I was advised by Ioannis Caragiannis.

My research interests lie at the intersection of theoretical computer science, artificial intelligence, and microeconomic theory. I am highly interested in the design and analysis of simple algorithms for fundamental problems in algorithmic game theory and computational social choice.
